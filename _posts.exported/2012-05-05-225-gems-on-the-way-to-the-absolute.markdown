<p><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';"></span><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';"></span>Created on Sunday 7 April 2013 18:46</p>

<p class="subNoteDisc"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">108 sentences selected and compiled from <a href="http://www.swami-krishnananda.org/realis_0.html" class="Next"><span class="Relatedlinks">The Realisation of the Absolute</span></a> on the occasion of Swami Krishnananda's 72nd birthday in 1994.</span></p>
<div id="discText"><ol>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Philosophy is the dear delight of the reason; the great joy of the understanding.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">True philosophy is the solace of the heart, the peace of the mind, the refuge of the human individual.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Philosophy examines the entire gamut of possible experiences and lifts human thought to the Divine Consciousness. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Only a citizen of the Universe can be an enjoyer of peace  the peace that passeth understanding.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Upanishads have always been acknowledged and acclaimed as veritable mines of Transcendental Wisdom.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">It is a mistake to be interested in the different forms of perception. Nothing is worth considering except the realisation of Brahman.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">This intellect is a very inadequate means of ascertaining Truth. But however much imperfect, it is the only human faculty of knowledge nearest to Reality.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">To express what is complete is not within the capacity of the knowing process. All knowing is a process, and all process is imperfection.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Intellect is never free from subject-object relationship  and every such relation falls short of Reality.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Reason should always be aided by tolerance, and should not forget its own limitations.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The only condition, however, is that the aspiring intellect should be pure and unattached.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even death occurs through wrong belief, and even life is saved through mere belief. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Perfection on Truth cannot be two, and there cannot be two Absolutes.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Existence is really the existence of Consciousness.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Nothing that is related to another is real. Relation always means interdependence and not Self-Existence.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even the emperorship of the entire Universe cannot give perpetual satisfaction as long as it falls short of the Infinite. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Upanishads are the ripe fruits of fine flowers blossomed out in the light of the Wisdom-Sun. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The quickness of the process of Attainment depends upon the intensity of the power of Meditation  both in its negative and assertive aspects.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Bliss of unlimited Consciousness is the Zenith of Existence, and every thing other than this is condemned as untrue.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The delight of the Self is the delight of Being. It is the Bliss of Consciousness-Absolute.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Change is the quality of untruth and the Upanishads assert that Reality is self-satisfied, self-existent, non-dual, tranquil and utterly Perfect.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Truth "Knowing which every thing becomes known" is the subject of enquiry and the object of quest in the Upanishads.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Blessed is he, and he has truly lived a purposed life, who attains to the height of undying joy in this very life; and he is a great loser and has lived his life in vain, who has failed to realise the Truth here. (Kena Up. 2-5)</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">All is well with him, whose heart is turned towards acting in accordance with the deathless law of Infinite Life. No disease, physical or mental, can ever assault him.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The welfare of society rests in its spirituality.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The ills caused by wrong methods of education, the social and political strife, the individual evils and the world-degeneration are all effected by the one terrible fact, that humanity turned against the law of the Spiritual Reality.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Upanishads are our guidelights in the Supreme Pursuit. Let us understand and follow them with sincerity, faith, calmness, surety and persistence. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Change is the law of life; nothing is without changing itself.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Tranquility can well be said to be non-existent in the history of the space-time world. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Cognition is impossible without a pre-existence link between the subject and the object.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">All contacts presuppose an immovable ground which supports all movements. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">That objects exist also cannot be proved unless there are minds to cognise and know them. Each is explained only by the other and not by itself. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The test of Reality is non-dependence, completeness and imperishability.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">One Reality appears as the knower as well as the known. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;"> The Substance by itself does not change; only the mode of perception changes. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">In order to have the experience of Reality we have to discard the forms as mere appearances.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">To assert diversity is to deny Absoluteness.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">To say that we are not yet the Reality, and we have yet to 'become' It, may be true with partiality to empirical Consciousness, but it is not the Highest Truth. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Realisation is not an actual 'becoming', but an unfolding of Consciousness, an Experience of Truth  Truth that already is, Truth that is eternal. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Self is not really bound by space and time. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Absolute of the Upanishads is the only Reality and all forms must, therefore, be nonexistent from the point of view of Its exact nature.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">A faithfulness to diversity must necessarily end in a failure in the practical walk of life. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Truth is the undivided Absolute. Truth cannot be twofold. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Absolute and the relative are not two different entities standing like father and son.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">If Brahman has expressed Itself as the world, then the world cannot exist outside Brahman.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even space is Brahman. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">If we are not Brahman at present, we can never be That at any time in future. A Not-Brahman cannot be turned into Brahman. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Absolute-Existence does not admit of differentiation of any kind.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Nothing can be said about the Absolute, except that It 'Is'.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Brahman which is the cause, and the world which is the effect are basically identical, and hence change and causation lose their meaning. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Absolutism satisfactorily solves all the problems of life.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Everyone is inside the prison of his own experience and knows nothing outside his consciousness. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">A God, who changes Himself, is not a permanent Being. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The richness of the part is not equal to the magnificence of the Whole.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The world 'All' does not refer to the reality of the plurality of things.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Duality cannot survive and individuality cannot exist in the Truth of Brahman. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The infinite Bhuma alone hails Supreme. It is established in Its own Greatness. It is not dependent on anything else, for anything else is not. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">All that appears to exist need not really exist as such. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Reaching the Real is not an action. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">We seem to be doing many things, though actually we do nothing. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">A perishable means cannot lead to an eternal End. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The world exists, because the mind functions on a dualistic basis. There is sound, because there is the ear and there is colour, because there is the eye. The human individual exists as such, because it thinks. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">No form is self-existent.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The dance of ideas is the world of experience.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Though no thing exists, it is not true, that nothing exists  for Consciousness exists. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">There is no duality. All modification is illusory. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The form of the world of plurality is an illusion, though the ultimate Essence of the world is real.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Truth persists even in the extreme of untruth. Untruth is a lesser truth and evil is a lesser degree of goodness. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The individual is the footprint of the Absolute. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The individual is a copy or miniature of the Cosmic.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Truth is inclusive of everything in the world. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Man begins from the physical body and ends in the imperishable Soul.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Life is a dramatic struggle for Self-Realisation, and Truth-Experience.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The state of perfection is neither an indivisibility nor a multiplicity,  but an indivisible Multiplicity.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The world is not an illusion, but a form of the Absolute.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even materialism is a step in the path to Perfection. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Death is the beginning of a better life. Evil is the starting point of a state leading to good. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Every thing is only a part of the Infinite Completeness.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">We cannot know any thing except in terms of what we are. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The knowledge of everything through the knowledge of One Thing implies that everything is made up of that One Thing.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Thought is objectified consciousness. The greater the objectification, the denser is the ignorance and the acuter are the pains suffered. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The aspiration of every living being is to find rest in the blissful possession of Eternal Life and nothing short of it. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The march of the soul is from the false to the true, from the apparent to the real, from the shadow to the light, from the perishable to the ever-enduring. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">There is nothing greater than or equal to the knowledge of the Aatman : "Aatmalaabhaat Na Param Vidyate".</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">When we turn our face away from this One Reality, we open the door to Self-imprisonment.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The love of life is based on the love of the Self. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The fervour of a Nachiketas or Dhruva, of a Prahlaada or Meera is expected in every spiritual aspirant.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even Devarshi Narada's knowledge is regarded by Sanatkumara as "mere names, mere words".</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Even death is not a bar in the process of the Realisation of Truth. Death is a reshuffling of Consciousness to adjust and adopt itself to a different order of life. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">To know Him is to be saved. Not to know Him is death. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The ordinary man of the world has his mind and senses turned extrovert.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;"> Some blessed one turns his gaze inward and beholds the glorious light of the Self. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The Self is imperishable. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Consciousness gets diffused through the distractive intellect and creates the perception of multiplicity.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Forms float in Truth, even as bubbles in the ocean. They cannot exist apart from the Ocean of Truth. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The deceived Soul fears death of its body, death of what it considers as dear. It loves objects, which do not promise real satisfaction. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The dream-objects have to vanish if waking experience is to be had. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Every true civilisation, if it is not meant to deceive itself, has to gird up its loins for Self-Realisation. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The value of a person is nothing if he does not aspire for the realisation of the Eternal Good, the Good not merely of this or that class of men, but of the entire Universe. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Perfection is Absolute-Experience, 'Brahma-Anubhava', the Consciousness of Reality. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Omnipresence, Omniscience and Omnipotence are said to be the characteristics of God. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Brahman is That, which is permanent in things that change. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The whole Universe is a spiritual Unity and is One with the essential Brahman. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The knowledge of the Self is the knowledge of Brahman. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">When Brahman is known, all is known. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">There is no seer but That, no hearer but That, no thinker but That, no knower but That.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">According to the Rig Veda, even "immortality and death are It's shadows". What ever truly exists is the Real.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Brahman is Infinite, the Universe is Infinite; from the Infinite proceeds the Infinite, and after deducting the Infinite from the Infinite, what remains is but the Infinite.</span></li>
</ol></div>
