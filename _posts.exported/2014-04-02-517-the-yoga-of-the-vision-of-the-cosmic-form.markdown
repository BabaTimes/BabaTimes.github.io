<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: center;" align="center"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">The Yoga of The Vision of the Cosmic Form</b></span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: center;" align="center"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">Divine Life Society Publication: - </b><a href="http://www.dlshq.org/download/bgita.htm#_VPID_20"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">Bhagavadgita  Summary of Eleventh Discourse</b></a><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;"> by Sri Swami Sivananda</b></span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;">Arjunas doubts having been removed through a clear description of the nature of the Atman and the origin and destruction of all created things, he is now ready to behold the Cosmic Vision.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;">Krishna grants him the divine sight by means of which Arjuna beholds the Lord as the vast Cosmic Manifestation. The vision is at once all-comprehensive and simultaneous. In every direction Arjuna sees the Lord as the entire universe. All the created worlds, gods, beings, creatures and things stand revealed as the one gigantic body of the Lord.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;">Arjuna further sees that the great cosmic drama is set in motion and controlled by the all-mighty power of the Lord. His Will alone prevails in all things and actions, both good and bad. The Lord exhorts him to fight, he being only an apparent cause of the destruction of his enemies.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;">Arjuna is unable to bear the pressure of the sudden expansion of consciousness and is filled with fear. He begs the Lord to assume once more His usual form.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing" style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: book antiqua,palatino;">Krishna reiterates that this vision cannot be had through any amount of austerities, study, sacrifices or philanthrophic acts. Supreme devotion is the only means by which one can have access to His grand vision.</span></p>
