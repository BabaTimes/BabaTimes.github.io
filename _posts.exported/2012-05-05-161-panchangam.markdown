<p><span style="font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;"><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';">A panchngam is a Hindu astrological almanac, which follows traditional Indian cosmology, and presents important astronomical data in tabulated form. It is sometimes spelled <i>Pancanga</i>, <i>Panchanga</i>, <i>Panchaanga</i>, or <i>Panchnga</i>, and is pronounced <i>Panchnga</i>. Pachangas are used in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jyotisha" title="Jyotisha"><span style="color: #000000;">Jyotisha</span></a> (<span class="unicode"><span title="International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration">Jyoti</span></span><span class="unicode"><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';">s</span></span><span class="unicode"><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';">a</span></span></span><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif';">).</span></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">In Eastern India, including Assam, Bengal, <span style="color: #000000;">Orisa</span> the Panchangam is referred to as <span style="color: #000000;">Panjika</span>.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">Panchngas are published in India by many learned authors, societies, academies, and universities. Different publications differ only minutely, at least for a casual or un-trained reader. They forecast celestial phenomena such as solar eclipses, forecast weather (rain, dryspells) as well as more mundane occurrences.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">The study of Panchngas involves understanding <i>Rasi phala</i> (also pronounced 'Rashi phala'), the impact of the signs of the zodiac on the individual. Astrologers consult the Panchngam to set auspicious dates for weddings, corporate mergers, and other worldly activities as per religion.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">The actual casting of a Panchnga involves elaborate mathematical work involving high level of spherical geometry and sound understanding of astronomical phenomena, such as sidereal movements of heavenly bodies. However, in practice the tabulation is done on the basis of short-cut formulations as propounded by ancient Vedic sages and scholars.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">A typical Panchnga may state tabulations of positions of Sun, Moon, and other planets for every day of the year on a fixed place (longitude, latitude) and time of day (in 24-hour format IST). The users calculate the remaining data using the their relative difference from this fixed place and time.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">There are several panchngas that contain information for more than one year. There is one Vishvavijaya Panchnga that is for 100 years.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">The theories propounded in the two scriptures, Surya Siddhanta and <span style="color: #000000;">Grahalaghava</span> formed the basis for the myriad calendars or Panchngas in the past in different regions of the country - a culturally complex system.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">The Grahalaghava was compiled some 600 years ago and Surya Siddhanta was available ages before that. But these had become outdated and did not tally with actual astronomical events and did not tally with each other also. Hence, a committee was appointed by the Government of India with experts in the field drawn from various parts of the country who were involved with preparation of Panchnga in local languages to draw up a reliable Panchnga in which the mathematical calculations provides the positions of grahas (the planets) and nakshatras (constellations) in the sky as they are observed.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; font-size: 12pt; color: #000000;">Thus, the Government of India has prepared the National Panchnga or the Indian national calendar in 1957 (was proposed by Saha and <span style="color: #000000;">Lahiri</span> in 1952), which is used in predictive astrology. The Lahiris Ephemeris published annually is the most widely used English almanac in Vedic astrology apart from the many Panchngas published in local languages, which are mostly based on the National Panchnga.</span></p>
